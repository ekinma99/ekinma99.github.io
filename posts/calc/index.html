Why Calculus was hard for me

January 14, 2021

As a Computer Science major, I had to take two* semesters of Calculus. It wasn’t a fun experience but I got through.

Math Has Always Been a Bit of a Struggle

Math hasn’t always been easy for me but for some reason when I came into Calculus 1, I thought I would be ok. I did have Algebra 1, Algebra 2, Math Analysis, College Algebra, and Precalculus under my belt. I should be fine, right. Boy, was I wrong.

Calculus: Mostly Algebra with some Calculus Concepts

Calculus was hard for me for a number of reasons. One, I didn’t have a strong foundation in Algebra. I was able to pass all my previous math classes with a B or higher but that didn’t mean I truly understood Algebra. In Calculus, you will encounter a number of fractions, where you will be asked to simplify. For a single problem, you often need to use multiple rules to find the derivative. You need to be able to properly identify functions or you’ll be walking toward the wrong path. There’s so much involved that without a strong Algebra foundation, you are likely to make errors, many in which you will find yourself asking, “How could I be so stupid not to realize?” or “How could I have made that mistake?”


Wish I Realized the Truth Early On

I failed to realize that Calculus requires lots of practice especially when I didn’t have that strong foundation. It was hard getting through the homework since the homework contained difficult problems. I would spend hours stuck on a question, trying several similar questions but still no luck. Unfortunately, not being able to answer these homework questions led me to doing poorly on my exams.

My Professor Worked Us Good


In addition, the course was difficult because it required more work than I was used to. In my previous courses, I would have assignments once a week or every two weeks. In my Calculus courses, I was required to read before class, complete the reading assignments for each section, do Webassign, and complete two projects. Maybe it doesn't seem that bad, but for a course where the content was already hard, it was a lot.

Conclusion

I wasn’t prepared for this class. If I could go back and time, I would tell my high school self to spend part of my summer break going over Algebra. It was a lesson learned not to be overconfident and practice will get you through.

* I did more than 2 semesters. The exact number number doesn’t matter.
